How it works

Live in under a minute

Install both apps

Fonocam on your Android phone, Fonocam Desktop on your Windows PC. Both are single downloads below.

Press Start, then Connect

Start streaming on the phone — it pops up in the desktop app's device list on the same WiFi. Click Connect.

Pick the camera in any app

Hit Start Virtual Webcam and choose the camera in Zoom, Meet or OBS. That's it — you're live.

FAQ

Questions, answered

Is Fonocam really free?

Yes — everything you see today is free and open source. A one-time Pro upgrade with extras like full-HD output may come later, but the core app stays free.

Do I need OBS Studio?

Only its virtual camera driver, which installs with OBS Studio (free). You don't need to run or learn OBS — Fonocam just uses the driver so other apps can see your phone as a camera.

The video lags on WiFi. What can I do?

Use 720p with Medium quality for the smoothest experience, keep the phone near your router — or plug in a USB cable, which is faster than WiFi and charges the phone while you stream.

Does it work when the phone screen is off?

Streaming runs as a background service with a notification. Use the built-in battery saver (screen dims but stays on) for the most reliable long sessions — some phone brands pause the camera if the screen turns fully off.

How do updates work?

The Android app checks GitHub for new releases — Settings → Check for update, then one tap to download. For the desktop app, grab the latest .exe from the same releases page.
